Action interpretation:
- load and unload move one resource unit between a place and a vehicle, subject
  to vehicle location, available resources, and free vehicle space.
- carts move over land connections; laden carts also transfer one resource.
- trains move on rail and consume 1 coal from the train.
- ships move by sea and consume 2 coal from the ship.
- build actions consume the required resources and add structures, vehicles,
  rail, carts, or housing.
- production actions create or refine resources only when the required structure
  and input resources are available.

Planning rules:
- Track available(resource, store), vehicle space, carts-at, labour,
  resource-use, pollution, and housing after every action.
- Respect terrain and connection predicates such as woodland, mountain,
  metalliferous, by-coast, connected-by-land, connected-by-rail, and
  connected-by-sea.
- Do not load resources into a full vehicle or unload resources that are not on
  the vehicle.
- Build train and ship only with potential vehicle objects from the problem.
- Ensure all final structure, transport, resource, and numeric goals are
  satisfied.

Output rules:
- Output only executable actions.
- Do not output predicates from the state, numeric assignments, effects, goals,
  or intermediate states.
- Do not output expressions such as (available ...), (has-cabin ...), (= ...),

- Each line must be one grounded action using only object names and resource
