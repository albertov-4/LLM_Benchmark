TASK FAMILY: rover

You are solving a PDDL planning problem with numeric rover energy.

The domain models rovers navigating between waypoints, collecting soil and rock
samples, taking images, and communicating data to a lander. Rovers consume
energy for navigation, sampling, calibration, imaging, and communication.

Use only the action schemas defined in the domain:
- navigate
- recharge
- sample_soil
- sample_rock
- drop
- calibrate
- take_image
- communicate_soil_data
- communicate_rock_data
- communicate_image_data

Action interpretation:
- navigate moves a rover between visible, traversable waypoints and costs 8
  energy.
- recharge increases rover energy by 20 at sunny waypoints when energy is at
  most 80.
- sample_soil and sample_rock require the correct equipment, a matching sample
  at the current waypoint, an empty store, and enough energy.
- drop empties a full store for later reuse.
- calibrate prepares a camera for imaging and costs 2 energy.
- take_image requires a calibrated onboard camera, a supported mode, visibility,
  and enough energy; taking an image removes calibration.
- communicate actions require collected data, visibility to a lander, a free
  channel, and enough energy.

Planning rules:
- Track rover locations, energy, store empty/full state, samples, images, and
  communicated data after every action.
- Recharge only at waypoints with in_sun.
- Use drop when a rover store must be freed before collecting another sample.
- Recalibrate a camera after take_image if another image is needed.
- Ensure all final communicated data goals are satisfied.

Output rules:
- Output only executable actions.
- Do not output predicates from the state, numeric assignments, effects, goals,
  or intermediate states.
- Do not output expressions such as (in ...), (have_image ...), (= ...),
  (increase ...), or (decrease ...).
- Each line must be one grounded action using only object names from the problem.
