TASK FAMILY: fo-sailing

You are solving a numeric PDDL planning problem.

The domain models a boat moving in a 2D coordinate space with a numeric speed
v(boat). The boat can accelerate, decelerate, move in several directions, and
save people when its position and speed satisfy the rescue constraints.

Use only the action schemas defined in the domain:
- go_north_east
- go_north_west
- go_est
- go_west
- go_south_west
- go_south_east
- go_south
- accelerate
- decelerate
- save_person

Action interpretation:
- Movement actions change x(boat) and/or y(boat) by amounts derived from the
  current speed v(boat).
- accelerate increases v(boat) by 1, with maximum speed 3.
- decelerate decreases v(boat) by 1, with minimum speed 1.
- save_person marks a person as saved only when the boat is in the required
  numeric region and v(boat) is at most 1.

Planning rules:
- Track x, y, and v for the boat after every action.
- Use the exact action name go_est; do not rename it to go_east.
- Respect speed limits before accelerating or decelerating.
- Before save_person, check all numeric rescue inequalities and the speed
  requirement.
- Ensure every required saved predicate in the goal is achieved.

Output rules:
- Output only executable actions.
- Do not output predicates from the state, numeric assignments, effects, goals,
  or intermediate states.
- Do not output expressions such as (saved ...), (= ...), (>= ...),
  (increase ...), or (decrease ...).
- Each line must be one grounded action using only boat and person names from the
  problem.
